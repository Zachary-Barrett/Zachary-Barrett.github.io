Chicken Tacos

After Spaniards came to Mesoamerica in the early 1500s, a great deal of new food and cooking techniques were introduced to the natives. Spaniards themselves also had a multitude of influences in their own cooking from the Mediterranean area, allowing them to show these skills to the people that were living in the Americas at the time. With new ingredients and new techniques to use, the creation of some of the earliest popular Mexican dishes began to occur. Spanish Colonization - domesticated chicken Some of the most significant foods introduced to the natives were meats from domesticated animals.

This is a very simple recipe for Shredded chicken tacos that are easy to make and even easier to enjoy.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup Water
  • 1(1 ounce packet) of taco seasoning
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 2 tbsp oregano
  • 1/2 tsp white sugar
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 Lbs Shredded Chicken
  • Mini flour or corn tortilla's (Your choice)
  • 3 Avocado
  • Tobasco
  • Cotija
  • Sour Cream
  • Shredded Lettuce
  • Himalayan Pink Salt

Directions

  1. Mix water, taco seasoning, gralic, oregano, white sugar, and tobasco in a bowl
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large pan on high heat. Add the shredded chicken and cook for about 8-minutes
  3. Add the sauce mixture created in step 1 to the chicken and cook for 18 more minutes
  4. In a seperate bowl mix Avocado with himalayan pink slat and Tobasco
  5. Add the avocado mixture, sour cream, the cojita cheese, and shredded iceberg lettuce to a tortilla and put your chicken on there. Then eat and enjoy
